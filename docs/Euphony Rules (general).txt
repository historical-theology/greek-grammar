ELISION Smyth §69

A short vowel at the end of a word may drop out when the following word begins with a vowel.
	-An apostrophe is put in place of the elided vowel
The final vowel of an emphatic personal pronoun is rarely elided
Interior elision occurs in the forming of compound words (ουδε + εις -> ουδεις)
	-An apostrophe is not used.

Interior Elision does not occure when:
	Monosyllables (except those that end in ε [e.g. τέ, δέ, γέ])
	The conjunction ὀτι
	The prepositions προ, αρχι, μεχρι, περί
	The dative singular ending ι of the third declension, and in σι (ending of the dative plural)
	Forms admitting movable ν (except εστι)
